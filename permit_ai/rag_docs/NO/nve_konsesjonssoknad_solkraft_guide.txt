# Solar Power Concession Application Guide — Norway (NVE)

## Overview

NVE (Norges vassdrags- og energidirektorat) provides a comprehensive digital guide for solar power plant concession applications. Most ground-mounted solar power plants require construction licensing under the Energy Act (energiloven).

## Mandatory Application Components

Applications must include:

1. Introduction section
2. Project description covering the full installation
3. Environmental and societal impacts assessment
4. Required attachments (maps, technical specifications)

## Required Assessment Topics

Applicants must address all of the following:

- Natural hazards and climate adaptation measures
- Aquatic ecosystems and fish populations
- Wildlife, invasive species, and plant pests
- Water and groundwater contamination
- Network connection and grid capacity
- Light reflection impacts from solar panels
- Electronic communication infrastructure effects
- Land use and component descriptions
- Summary of key figures

## Key Regulatory Updates

- February 2025: Added references to guidance for power-sensitive information; clarified requirements for wildlife, invasive species, contamination, and climate assessment; required digital map files for grid connection
- June 1, 2025: Solar power plant notifications became subject to mandatory reporting requirements
- July/April 2024: Added requirements for key figures summary and descriptions of planning areas, land use, and components
- April 2026: Public health removed as a distinct assessment category

## Application Process

1. Submit konsesjonssøknad (concession application) to NVE
2. NVE conducts public consultation (høring)
3. Assessment of environmental and grid impacts
4. NVE issues concession (konsesjon) or rejection

## Grid Connection

Applications must address network connection (nettilknytning) including:
- Connection point and grid capacity
- Transmission/distribution system requirements
- Digital map files of proposed connection
