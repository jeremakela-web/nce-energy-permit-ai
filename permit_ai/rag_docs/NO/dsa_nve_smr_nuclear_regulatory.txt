# Norway SMR Nuclear Regulatory Framework (2024–2026)

## Current Status

Norway has no operating nuclear power plants but is actively developing its regulatory framework for SMR (Small Modular Reactor) technologies.

## Key Regulatory Event (2024)

Nuclear project developer Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a proposal to Norway's Ministry of Energy in November 2023 for an SMR plant in the municipalities of Aure and Heim. Norway's Ministry of Energy published a notification with a proposal for an EIA assessment programme in May 2024, with public consultation.

## Responsible Authorities

Three authorities jointly set the framework for nuclear EIA assessment in Norway:
- **DSA** (Direktoratet for strålevern og atomsikkerhet / Norwegian Radiation and Nuclear Safety Authority) — radiation and nuclear safety
- **NVE** (Norges vassdrags- og energidirektorat) — energy licensing under the Energy Act
- **DSB** (Direktoratet for samfunnssikkerhet og beredskap) — civil protection

## Legal Basis

- **Atomenergiloven** (Atomic Energy Act) — DSA issues licenses with conditions
- **Energiloven** (Energy Act) — NVE handles concession for power plant construction
- **Plan- og bygningsloven** — municipal planning requirements

## DSA Licensing Conditions

DSA has prepared a booklet with general terms and conditions for licences under the Atomic Energy Act. A guide elaborates on these terms, based on IAEA framework. DSA's guidelines are not legally binding in themselves but can be made binding through conditions in the license.

## Government Review (2024–2026)

In June 2024, the Norwegian government appointed a committee for a broad review of nuclear power establishment in Norway. The committee's report was due April 1, 2026. A new National Strategy for Radiation Protection and Nuclear Safety was adopted in 2025.

## IAEA Recognition

IAEA has recognized Norway's commitment to strengthen nuclear and radiation safety through DSA's regulatory framework.

## SMR Authorization Process (Proposed)

For SMR projects, the anticipated process involves:
1. Proposal to Ministry of Energy
2. Environmental Impact Assessment (konsekvensutredning) under DSA/NVE/DSB framework
3. Concession under Energiloven (NVE)
4. Nuclear facility license under Atomenergiloven (DSA)
5. Building permit under Plan- og bygningsloven
