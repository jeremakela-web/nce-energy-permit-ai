# Poland Electrical Energy Storage (BESS) Regulations — URE Report

## Regulatory Framework (Since 2021)

Since July 3, 2021, the storage of electricity in storage facilities with total installed capacity exceeding 10 MW is a regulated activity subject to a licence from URE (Urząd Regulacji Energetyki — Energy Regulatory Office).

Storage facilities with capacity between 50 kW and 10 MW must be registered with network operators but do not require licenses.

## Grid Connection Rules

Distribution system operators (DSOs) face ownership limitations under Polish law:
- "An electricity system operator is not allowed to own, build, operate or manage energy storage facilities" unless URE President grants special approval
- Special approval recognizes the facility as a Fully Integrated Network Element (FINE)
- Between February 2023 and May 2024: 6 distribution operators submitted 41 applications for FINE status

## Grid Connection Conditions (Warunki Przyłączenia)

Recent regulations changed connection condition validity:
- Validity period shortened from 2 years to 1 year (2024 reform)
- This compresses the development timeline for BESS investors from 24 months to 12 months
- Connection conditions (warunki przyłączenia) must be obtained from the relevant network operator (DSO or PSE for transmission level)

## Market Status (May 2024)

- 12 electricity storage facilities registered with total installed capacity of 1,464.5 MW
- Pumped storage plants: 85% of registered capacity
- Battery technology: approx. 50% of storage facilities
- Dominant battery technology: lithium-ion

## Cable Pooling (Cable Pooling) — 2023 Onwards

Energy storage is included in cable pooling, allowing PV + wind + BESS to share a single connection point. From April 2026, storage no longer counts as a separate source occupying connection capacity — this reduces grid connection costs for hybrid projects.

## Capacity Market

- 2028 capacity auction: storage = 15% of contracted capacity (vs. <7% previously)
- Total contracted capacity 2021-2028: 9.5 GW

## New Rules from 2026

Poland's Grid Act (Ustawa sieciowa) from 2026 changes RES and BESS grid connection rules, including:
- Simplified connection procedures for storage collocated with renewable installations
- Updated rules for hybrid RES+BESS projects

## Applicable Legislation

- Prawo energetyczne (Energy Law) — licensing, DSO restrictions
- Ustawa o odnawialnych źródłach energii (RES Law) — support mechanisms
- Ustawa sieciowa (Grid Act, 2026) — updated connection rules
- Rozporządzenie o warunkach przyłączenia — grid connection conditions regulation
