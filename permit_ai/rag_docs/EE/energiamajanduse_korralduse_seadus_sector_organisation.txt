ENERGIAMAJANDUSE KORRALDUSE SEADUS — ENERGY SECTOR ORGANISATION ACT (ESOS)
Source: Riigiteataja RT I, 28.06.2007, 23 (original); consolidated version with 2024 amendments
Authority: Riigikogu (Parliament of Estonia) / Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium
         (Ministry of Economic Affairs and Communications)
Language: Estonian (summary in English for RAG indexing)

=== PART 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE ===

The Energy Sector Organisation Act (Energiamajanduse korralduse seadus, ESOS) establishes:
1. The organizational framework and responsibilities of state bodies in the energy sector
2. Strategic energy planning obligations and energy targets
3. National energy security requirements
4. Obligations for energy efficiency and demand management
5. Framework for renewable energy support schemes

ESOS applies to all energy carriers: electricity, natural gas, oil, heat, and renewables. It works in conjunction with the Electricity Market Act (ETS) and the Natural Gas Act.

=== PART 2: NATIONAL ENERGY AND CLIMATE PLAN (NECP) ===

§§ 4-12 — National Energy Development Plan (Energiamajanduse riiklik arengukava):
Estonia is required to prepare and submit a National Energy and Climate Plan (NECP) to the European Commission under EU Regulation 2018/1999 (Governance Regulation). The NECP 2030 sets binding and indicative targets for Estonia:

Renewable Energy Targets (from NECP 2030 and RRP):
- Share of renewables in final energy consumption: 42% by 2030 (EU target)
- Electricity sector: aim for 100% renewable electricity by 2030
- Wind power: approximately 1,200 MW installed capacity by 2030 (onshore + offshore)
  - Baseline (2020): approximately 310 MW installed onshore wind
  - Recovery and Resilience Plan (RRP) target: unlock priority development areas
- Solar PV: approximately 415 MW installed capacity by 2030
  - Baseline (2020): approximately 100 MW
- Offshore wind: long-term target (post-2030) of several GW in the Gulf of Finland

These targets are incorporated into the ESOS framework and give legal backing to the renewable energy permitting acceleration measures.

§§ 13-20 — Energy Security:
- Obligations to maintain fuel reserves
- Interconnection capacity requirements with neighboring countries
- Emergency planning for energy supply disruptions

=== PART 3: ENERGY SECTOR ACTORS AND ROLES ===

§§ 21-35 — Government and Regulatory Roles:

Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium (Ministry of Economic Affairs and Communications, MKM):
- Primary ministry responsible for energy policy
- Prepares energy legislation and NECP
- Coordinates renewable energy auctions and support scheme design
- Issues general authorizations for energy market participants

Konkurentsiamet (Competition Authority / Energy Regulatory Authority):
- Regulates electricity and gas markets
- Issues electricity production licenses (elektrienergia tootmise luba)
- Approves tariffs for regulated monopolies (Elering, Elektrilevi, etc.)
- Supervises market conduct and prevents anti-competitive behavior
- Resolves disputes between market participants and network operators
- Reports to MKM and Riigikogu (Parliament)

Elering AS:
- TSO (Transmission System Operator)
- Maintains system balance
- 10-year network development planning
- Operates the metering system and registers market participants
- Coordinates with Baltic and Nordic TSOs

Elektrilevi OÜ:
- Largest distribution system operator (DSO) in Estonia
- Operates distribution network below 110 kV
- Other DSOs include Imatra Elekter and regional operators

§§ 36-45 — Energy Service Providers:
- Balance responsible parties (bilansivastutajad): must register with Elering
- Electricity traders (elektrimüüjad): must hold trading license
- Energy auditors (energiaaudiitorid): certified professionals for energy audits

=== PART 4: RENEWABLE ENERGY AUCTIONS ===

§§ 56-70 — Support Scheme for Renewables (Taastuvenergia toetuse skeem):
The Ministry of Economic Affairs and Communications administers renewable energy competitive auctions:

Auction mechanism:
- Competitive bidding for contracts for difference (CfD) or premium tariffs
- Available for wind, solar, and other eligible renewable technologies
- BESS co-located with renewables may qualify for support in future rounds

2024 auction parameters (reference):
- Technology-neutral competitive auction
- Strike price based on competitive bids
- 15-year support period
- Grid connection required before support commencement

Offshore wind state aid scheme:
- EU-approved state aid package of approximately €2.6 billion for Estonian offshore wind (European Commission approval 2024)
- Enables long-term CfD support for offshore wind projects
- Critical for project financing of offshore wind in Estonian territorial waters and EEZ

§§ 71-80 — Net metering (netomõõtmine):
For small-scale solar PV:
- Households (< 15 kW): net metering available — excess production sold to grid at spot price
- Commercial buildings (< 50 kW): simplified net metering
- No production license required; registration notification (teatis) to Konkurentsiamet sufficient

=== PART 5: ENERGY EFFICIENCY OBLIGATIONS ===

§§ 81-100 — Energy Efficiency (Energiatõhususe seadus framework):
Large energy consumers (including data centers, industrial facilities) are subject to:
- Energy efficiency audits every 4 years (for enterprises > 250 employees or > €50M turnover)
- Implementation of EU Energy Efficiency Directive 2012/27/EU as amended by 2018/2002/EU
- Building energy performance certificates for all sold or rented buildings
- District heating efficiency requirements

For data centers specifically:
- Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD) compliance for new construction
- Green data center certification schemes encouraged (though not legally mandatory in Estonia)
- Connection to waste heat recovery systems may be required by municipalities

=== PART 6: CROSS-BORDER AND BALTIC COORDINATION ===

§§ 101-115 — Regional Energy Cooperation:
- Baltic Energy Market Interconnection Plan (BEMIP): Estonia participates in joint Baltic energy market development with Latvia and Lithuania
- NordBalt and EstLink interconnectors: Estonia-Finland and Estonia-Latvia electricity cables already operational
- Baltic-Continental synchronization (BalticGrid): Estonia participates in the planned synchronization of Baltic electricity grids with Continental European Network (scheduled 2025)
- Joint Baltic renewable energy development: coordination of offshore wind in Baltic Sea through Baltic Energy Ministerial Forum

=== PART 7: SECTOR REVIEW AND REPORTING ===

§§ 116-130 — Monitoring and Reporting:
- Annual report on renewable energy progress to European Commission
- NECP progress reports (biennial)
- Elering publishes annual adequacy report
- Konkurentsiamet publishes annual regulatory report

=== PART 8: RELEVANCE TO PROJECT TYPES ===

All energy generation project types (onshore wind, offshore wind, solar PV, BESS, SMR):
- ESOS provides the policy and strategic framework
- National targets (1,200 MW wind, 415 MW solar by 2030) support project approvals
- Renewable energy support scheme (auctions, net metering) governed by ESOS
- EU-approved offshore wind state aid scheme (€2.6B, 2024) governed under ESOS framework

Data centers (datakeskus):
- Energy efficiency obligations under ESOS framework
- Connection to grid governed by ETS (Elering/Elektrilevi)
- No special sector-specific permits under ESOS

Industrial projects (teollisuus):
- Energy efficiency audit obligations for large consumers
- Potential for industrial self-generation under ETS framework
- District heating connection requirements in urban areas

Residential and commercial buildings (asuinrakennus, liikerakennus):
- Building energy performance certificates (ESOS / Building Energy Performance Act)
- Net metering for on-site solar PV generation
