=== PART 1: ELERING AS — ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES ===

Elering AS is Estonia's Transmission System Operator (TSO), responsible for:
- Operating and developing the 110 kV and 330 kV high-voltage transmission grid
- Balancing electricity supply and demand in real time
- Coordinating cross-border electricity flows with Latvia (through LitPol Link and Baltic synchronization)
- Managing capacity allocation for grid connections of generators and large consumers
- Publishing grid connection standards and processing connection applications
- Acting as the central registry for metering and balance settlement

Elering is the Estonian equivalent of Fingrid (Finland) and Statnett (Norway). Like these TSOs, Elering operates as a regulated monopoly under oversight of Konkurentsiamet (Competition Authority).

Elering's legal basis: Elektrituruseadus (ETS) §§10–13, EU Third Energy Package, and Baltic Energy Market Interconnection Plan (BEMIP).

=== PART 2: GRID CONNECTION PROCEDURE ===

Step 1 — Pre-connection Feasibility Study (Liitumiseelne uuring):
Before formal application, Elering recommends (and for large projects requires) a pre-connection feasibility study request. This study:
- Identifies the nearest suitable grid connection point (ühenduspunkt)
- Assesses grid capacity at that point
- Estimates connection costs and reinforcement requirements
- Provides a preliminary timeline

Fee: charged based on study complexity; typically €1,000–€10,000 for large generators.
Timeframe: 4–8 weeks for the feasibility study.

Step 2 — Connection Application (Liitumistaotlus):
Formal application to Elering includes:
- Technical description of the generation or storage facility
- Installed capacity (MW) and expected annual generation (MWh)
- Grid connection voltage level requested (110 kV or 330 kV)
- Proposed connection point location
- Environmental permits obtained (or status)
- Building permit status
- Timeline for commissioning

Step 3 — Technical Connection Conditions (Liitumistingimused):
Elering issues technical connection conditions (TCCs) specifying:
- Required equipment specifications for the connection
- Protection relay settings
- Metering requirements
- Power quality compliance (voltage, frequency, harmonics — per IEC 61000 series)
- Reactive power capability requirements
- Active power control and ramp rate requirements
- Fault ride-through (FRT) requirements per EU Network Code RfG 2016/631

Step 4 — Connection Agreement (Liitumisleping):
After technical conditions are agreed, Elering and the applicant sign a connection agreement. This agreement specifies:
- Connection point and voltage level
- Technical specifications to be met
- Connection fee (regulated tariff)
- Timeline for Elering's network reinforcement works (if needed)
- Commercial operation date target

Step 5 — Construction and Testing:
- Generator builds the connection infrastructure from the facility to the grid connection point
- Elering inspects and approves the connection infrastructure
- Pre-commissioning tests conducted per Elering's test protocol
- Synchronization approval from Elering before first energization

Step 6 — Commercial Connection (Kommertsiaalühendus):
After successful pre-commissioning tests, Elering approves commercial operation.

=== PART 3: TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS FOR GENERATORS ===

Technical requirements are based on EU Network Codes (Commission Regulations):
- 2016/631/EU — Network Code on Requirements for Grid Connection of Generators (RfG)
- 2016/1447/EU — HVDC connections (applies to offshore wind via HVDC cables)
- 2016/1388/EU — Demand Connection Code (for large consumers, including data centers)

Power categories (Type A, B, C, D) per RfG:
- Type A: < 0.8 kW (household PV, trivial connections)
- Type B: 0.8 kW – < 1 MW (small generators)
- Type C: 1 MW – < 75 MW (most utility-scale solar, onshore wind, BESS)
- Type D: ≥ 75 MW (large power plants, major wind farms)

Type C and D generators must comply with:
1. Frequency response: automatic response to frequency deviations within 0.5 Hz
2. Reactive power: capability to provide reactive power within specified Q-P diagram
3. Fault ride-through (FRT): remain connected during voltage dips for specified durations
4. Ramp rate control: limit active power ramp rates as instructed by Elering
5. Active power control: respond to Elering dispatch instructions (for system balancing)
6. Monitoring and communication: SCADA connection to Elering's control system (RTU/IEC 60870-5-104)

For offshore wind (HVDC connected):
Additional requirements under Commission Regulation 2016/1447:
- DC protection and pole blocking
- AC side performance requirements (equivalent to Type D onshore)
- Black start capability may be required

=== PART 4: ELERING NETWORK DEVELOPMENT PLAN 2024–2033 ===

Elering's 10-year network development plan (Arengukava 2024–2033) identifies:

Priority investments:
1. Baltic synchronization: completion of synchronization of Estonian, Latvian, and Lithuanian grids with Continental European Network (planned desynchronization from Russian IPS/UPS by 2025). This requires significant reinforcement investments.
2. Offshore wind connection: Elering is planning new 110–330 kV submarine cable connections to support offshore wind development in the Gulf of Finland and Baltic Sea.
3. Onshore wind and solar integration: identification of "connection capacity areas" (ühendusmahtuvuse piirkonnad) where new generation can connect without major reinforcement.

Relevant for new projects:
- New onshore wind and solar PV in areas with existing 110 kV grid may connect with moderate reinforcement (costs borne partly by generator, partly by Elering).
- Areas remote from the 110 kV grid require significant cable/overhead line extensions; Elering assessment required.
- Offshore wind projects in priority development areas will benefit from planned offshore grid development (2025–2030).

=== PART 5: DISTRIBUTION NETWORK CONNECTION (ELEKTRILEVI) ===

For smaller generators (typically < 10 MW or connecting to 20 kV or lower):
Connection is made to the distribution network operated by Elektrilevi OÜ (Estonia's dominant DSO).

Elektrilevi connection procedure:
1. Application for connection conditions (liitumistingimuste taotlus)
2. Elektrilevi issues connection conditions (30-day statutory deadline)
3. Connection agreement (liitumisleping) signed
4. Construction and commissioning
5. Commercial connection

Technical requirements: EU Network Code RfG applies (Type A, B, or C category).
Metering: Standard smart meter installation by Elektrilevi.

Rooftop solar PV (< 15 kW): Simplified connection — Elektrilevi provides connection conditions; process typically < 30 days.

=== PART 6: CONNECTION FEES (REGULATED TARIFFS) ===

Elering's connection fees are regulated by Konkurentsiamet and published in Elering's price list.
Key principles:
- Deep connection costs (network reinforcement) are shared: first N km of reinforcement is borne by Elering; longer extensions paid by the connecting party.
- Point connection costs (bay equipment, cables from facility to grid) are borne by the connecting party.
- 2024 tariff schedule: available at elering.ee/liitumised

Typical fee ranges (indicative, 2024):
- 110 kV bay at Elering substation: €150,000–€500,000 depending on substation capacity
- New 110 kV cable/OHL construction: €100,000–€400,000 per km
- Total connection cost for 50 MW wind farm: €500,000–€5,000,000 depending on distance to grid

=== PART 7: SPECIAL CASES ===

Offshore Wind Connection:
- For offshore wind connected at 33 kV or 66 kV (AC collection): connection to Elering requires offshore or coastal substation with 110/330 kV transformer.
- For offshore wind via HVDC: direct DC connection to onshore converter station at Elering substation.
- Elering participates in Baltic Sea offshore grid coordination via ENTSO-E and Baltic Sea Energy Group.

Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS):
- BESS may connect to Elering transmission or Elektrilevi distribution depending on capacity.
- BESS must demonstrate both charging and discharging capacity meet technical requirements.
- No special BESS-specific connection tariff — standard generator tariff applies for discharging, standard consumer tariff for charging.
- Virtual power plant (VPP) aggregation is supported under Elering's balancing framework.

SMR (if future license granted):
- Would require 110 kV or 330 kV connection to Elering transmission grid.
- Elering technical requirements would apply as Type D generator.
- Special protection requirements for nuclear generation (loss-of-mains protection, islanding capability).

Data Centers:
- Typically connect to Elektrilevi distribution or Elering transmission depending on load size.
- Large data centers (> 10 MW load) may request direct 110 kV Elering transmission connection.
- Elering's DCC requirements (Commission Regulation 2016/1388) apply for demand connection.

=== PART 8: REGULATORY FRAMEWORK AND OVERSIGHT ===

Konkurentsiamet (Competition Authority, Estonia):
- Regulates Elering and Elektrilevi as natural monopolies
- Approves connection tariffs and methodology
- Resolves disputes between connecting parties and network operators
- Issues electricity production licenses (elektrienergia tootmise luba)

Energiamajanduse korralduse seadus (Energy Sector Organisation Act):
- Establishes the organizational framework for the energy sector
- Defines the roles of Elering, Konkurentsiamet, and other actors
- Sets energy sector targets and planning obligations

ENTSO-E membership:
Elering is a member of ENTSO-E (European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity) and applies ENTSO-E grid codes and planning standards.
