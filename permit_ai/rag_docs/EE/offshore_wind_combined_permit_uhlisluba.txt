OFFSHORE WIND COMBINED PERMIT (ÜHISLUBA) — ESTONIA
Source: Offshore Wind Development Reform (2023–2024)
        Amendments to: Elektrituruseadus (ETS), Keskkonnaseadustiku üldosa seadus (KeÜS),
        Majandusvööndi seadus (Exclusive Economic Zone Act), Planeerimisseadus (PlanS)
        EU State Aid SA.110117 (2024/N) — Estonia Offshore Wind Support Scheme
Authority: Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium, Keskkonnaamet, Elering AS
Language: Estonian (summary in English for RAG indexing)

=== PART 1: BACKGROUND AND REFORM ===

Estonia introduced a major reform of offshore wind permitting between 2022 and 2024, replacing the former three-step sequential permit process with a single Combined Permit (Ühisluba). This reform was part of:
1. Estonia's Recovery and Resilience Plan (RRP) commitments to streamline energy permitting
2. Implementation of the EU Renewable Energy Directive (RED II) requirements to reduce permitting timelines
3. Preparation for large-scale offshore wind development to meet national and EU climate targets

Former three-step process (pre-2023):
Step A: Zone reservation (tsoneerimine) — designation of offshore area in maritime spatial plan
Step B: Environmental Impact Assessment (KMH — Keskkonnamõju hindamine) — separate procedure
Step C: Construction permit / electricity production license — third separate procedure

Problems with former process:
- Cumulative timeline: 8–12 years from start to construction permit
- Duplications between steps (same information required multiple times)
- Unclear decision points and coordination between ministries
- Deterred investment in Estonian offshore wind despite excellent wind resource

=== PART 2: THE COMBINED PERMIT (ÜHISLUBA) ===

The Ühisluba (Combined Permit) is a single administrative procedure that integrates the following formerly separate approvals into one unified process:

1. Maritime Zone Designation (Tsoneerimine): approval of the use of the specific maritime area for offshore wind generation
2. Environmental Impact Assessment Approval (KMH heakskiit): approval of the EIA report within the combined process
3. Construction Permit (Ehitusluba / Rajamisluba): authorization to construct the offshore wind structures
4. Electricity Production License (Elektrienergia tootmise luba): authorization to operate as an electricity generator

The Ühisluba does NOT replace:
- Elering grid connection agreement (liitumisleping) — concluded separately with Elering
- Navigation/aviation safety approvals (Transpordiamet) — concurrent process
- Natura 2000 / habitats assessments (Keskkonnaamet) — integrated into EIA within combined process

Key features of the Ühisluba:
- Single application submitted to a lead authority (koordineeriv asutus)
- Lead authority: Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium (MKM) for offshore wind
- Statutory maximum timeline: 36 months from complete application to permit decision
  (with possibility of extension for complex EIA processes)
- All participating authorities (Keskkonnaamet, Transpordiamet, Elering, Kaitseministeerium, Veeteede Amet) give opinions within the combined process
- Public consultation: minimum 6-week consultation period for the combined application
- Appeals: administrative court (halduskohus) — one-stage appeals process

=== PART 3: APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS FOR ÜHISLUBA ===

An application for the Ühisluba must include:

Technical Documentation:
1. Project description: number, size, and type of turbines; total installed capacity (MW); hub height, rotor diameter, spacing
2. Location plan: precise coordinates of the wind farm footprint in the maritime zone
3. Grid connection plan: pre-connection study from Elering; proposed cable route from wind farm to onshore grid connection point
4. Construction method statement: foundation type (monopile, jacket, floating), installation sequence, transport routes
5. Decommissioning plan: scope and estimated cost of future decommissioning

Environmental Documentation (integrated EIA):
6. EIA scoping document (KMH programm): must be submitted first and approved by Keskkonnaamet before the main EIA
7. Full EIA report (KMH aruanne) including:
   - Baseline environmental survey (minimum 2 years of monitoring data recommended)
   - Ornithological impact assessment (bird migration studies — critical for Baltic Sea)
   - Marine ecology impact assessment (benthic habitats, marine mammals — especially harbour porpoise)
   - Visual impact assessment (viewshed analysis)
   - Noise impact (construction and operational noise modelling)
   - Electromagnetic interference assessment
   - Cumulative impact assessment with other planned projects

Safety and Navigation:
8. Maritime traffic safety assessment (IALA recommendations compliance)
9. Navigation light marking plan (Transpordiamet / Veeteede Amet requirements)
10. Aircraft warning lighting plan (Transpordiamet approval)
11. Military radar assessment (Kaitseministeerium pre-clearance strongly recommended before application)

Socio-economic:
12. Fisheries impact assessment and mitigation plan (coordination with fishing industry)
13. Local community consultation evidence
14. Economic impact assessment (employment, local supply chain)

Financial:
15. Evidence of financial capacity (project finance term sheets, equity commitments, or bank letter of comfort)
16. Decommissioning financial security plan

=== PART 4: EU STATE AID SCHEME €2.6 BILLION ===

European Commission Decision SA.110117 (2024/N) — Estonia Offshore Wind Support Scheme:
- Total budget: approximately €2.6 billion over the support period
- Form of support: Contracts for Difference (CfD) — two-way settlement mechanism
- Strike price: determined through competitive auction (competitive CfD)
- Support duration: 20 years from first electricity generation
- Eligible technology: offshore wind turbines in Estonian maritime zones
- Capacity target: approximately 1,000–2,000 MW offshore wind capacity to be supported

CfD mechanism:
- When market price < strike price: MKM/auction administrator pays the difference to the generator
- When market price > strike price: generator pays back the difference (two-way CfD)
- Payments based on actual metered generation
- Indexed to inflation to maintain real value of support

Auction design:
- Technology-specific (offshore wind only) competitive auction
- Selection criteria: strike price (primary), local content and supply chain commitments (secondary)
- Pre-qualification requirements: completed EIA, Ühisluba application submitted or Ühisluba obtained
- Timeline: first auction anticipated 2025/2026

Project economics significance:
- €2.6 billion state aid scheme provides long-term revenue certainty for project financing
- CfD structure eliminates electricity price risk for lenders
- Enables project finance (non-recourse) structures for Estonian offshore wind
- First EU-approved state aid for Estonian offshore wind — historic milestone for the market

=== PART 5: MARITIME SPATIAL PLANNING ===

Majandusvööndi seadus (Exclusive Economic Zone Act):
- Governs use of Estonian territorial sea and EEZ
- Estonian maritime zones available for offshore wind:
  - Territorial sea: 12 nautical miles from baseline
  - EEZ: up to 200 nautical miles (though Estonian EEZ is relatively small, bounded by Finland, Latvia, Russia)

Maritime spatial plan (Mereala planeering):
- Estonia adopted a national Maritime Spatial Plan (2023) identifying zones for:
  - Offshore wind development (priority areas identified in Gulf of Finland and Baltic Sea)
  - Nature protection zones (Natura 2000 marine sites — significant coverage)
  - Shipping lane preservation
  - Fisheries areas
  - Military exercise areas

Integration with Ühisluba:
- The Ühisluba process includes verification that the project is in a designated offshore wind zone in the maritime spatial plan
- Projects outside designated zones require amendment to the maritime spatial plan (additional procedure, significant delay)

=== PART 6: KEY AUTHORITIES FOR OFFSHORE WIND ===

Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium (MKM — Ministry of Economic Affairs):
- Lead authority for Ühisluba procedure
- Administers offshore wind CfD auctions
- Issues final Ühisluba decision

Keskkonnaamet (Environmental Board):
- Conducts EIA assessment within Ühisluba
- Natura 2000 / habitats assessment
- Noise and marine ecology assessment

Transpordiamet (Transport Administration):
- Maritime traffic safety (navigation lanes, warning lights)
- Aviation safety (aircraft warning lights on turbines)
- Civil aviation and maritime authority functions

Veeteede Amet (Maritime Administration / part of Transpordiamet):
- Waterway navigation safety
- Ice navigation requirements (Baltic Sea)

Kaitseministeerium (Ministry of Defence):
- Military radar interference clearance — CRITICAL
- Estonian Defence Forces operate coastal radar systems
- Pre-consultation essential; negative opinion can block project
- Mandatory military impact assessment for all offshore wind projects

Elering AS:
- TSO; provides pre-connection study
- Grid connection agreement (separate from Ühisluba but parallel process)
- Offshore cable route approval

Kalapüügiõiguste haldur (Fisheries authority):
- Fisheries impact assessment review
- Fishing exclusion zone negotiations

=== PART 7: TIMELINE FOR OFFSHORE WIND PROJECT UNDER NEW PROCESS ===

Phase 1 — Pre-development (12–24 months before Ühisluba application):
- Maritime spatial plan zone confirmation
- Wind and metocean resource measurement (minimum 1 year recommended)
- Baseline environmental survey initiation (birds, marine mammals, benthic habitats)
- Military radar pre-consultation with Kaitseministeerium
- Pre-connection feasibility study with Elering
- Stakeholder engagement (fishermen, local communities)

Phase 2 — EIA Scoping (3–6 months):
- KMH programm (scoping) submitted to Keskkonnaamet
- Public consultation on scoping document (2-week minimum)
- Keskkonnaamet issues approved scoping decision

Phase 3 — EIA and Ühisluba Application (12–18 months):
- Full EIA report preparation (parallel with Phase 2)
- Complete Ühisluba application submitted to MKM
- MKM declares application complete (validity check)
- Statutory consultation period begins: 6 weeks public consultation + concurrent agency consultations

Phase 4 — Assessment and Decision (12–18 months within the 36-month statutory deadline):
- MKM coordinates responses from Keskkonnaamet, Transpordiamet, Kaitseministeerium, etc.
- Public hearing (avalik arutelu) organized
- MKM issues Ühisluba (or rejection with reasons)

Phase 5 — Grid Connection (parallel with Phases 3–4):
- Elering connection conditions issued
- Grid connection agreement signed
- Onshore cable and substation works planned

Phase 6 — Construction (24–48 months):
- Procurement of turbines, foundations, cables
- Offshore construction (weather-dependent)
- Onshore substation construction

Phase 7 — Commercial Operation:
- Pre-commissioning tests with Elering
- Commercial connection approval
- CfD auction activation (if participating in support scheme)

Total timeline from start to commercial operation: 5–10 years (significantly shorter than pre-2023 process of 8–15 years).

=== PART 8: COMPARISON WITH FORMER 3-STEP PROCESS ===

Aspect                    | Former 3-Step Process       | New Ühisluba
--------------------------|-----------------------------|---------------------------
Number of procedures      | 3 separate                  | 1 integrated
Lead authority            | Multiple, unclear           | MKM (single point)
Maximum statutory timeline| No statutory max            | 36 months (statutory)
EIA placement             | Separate step               | Integrated into Ühisluba
Public consultation       | 3× separate rounds          | 1 unified round
Agency consultations      | Sequential                  | Concurrent
Overlap between phases    | Not allowed                 | Required (concurrent)
Typical total timeline    | 8–15 years                  | 4–8 years
Appeal mechanism          | Administrative + court      | Administrative court only
