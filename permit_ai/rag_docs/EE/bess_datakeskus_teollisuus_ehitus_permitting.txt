=== PART 1: BATTERY ENERGY STORAGE SYSTEMS (BESS) — AKUENERGIASALVESTUS ===

REGULATORY NOTE: No dedicated BESS-specific legislation exists in Estonia as of 2024.
General electricity market framework (Elektrituruseadus) and building regulations (Ehitusseadustik) apply.
Flag in reports: "General electricity market framework applies; no dedicated BESS regulation found."

Applicable legislation:
1. Elektrituruseadus (ETS): electricity production/storage license if discharging > 1 MW
2. Ehitusseadustik (EhS): building permit required for BESS containers/buildings
3. Kemikaaliseadus (Chemicals Act): lithium batteries classified as hazardous chemicals above certain thresholds; notification to Keskkonnaamet required for large installations
4. Jäätmeseadus (Waste Act): battery waste management obligations (battery end-of-life)
5. Tuleohutusseadus (Fire Safety Act): fire safety plan required for large BESS installations
6. KeÜS / KMH-KSH seadus: EIA screening if significant environmental impact (large installations in sensitive areas)

Permitting process for utility-scale BESS (> 1 MW):

Step 1 — Site and Zoning:
- Check land use designation: BESS may require detailplaneering if land not already zoned for energy/industrial use
- Municipality consultation recommended before planning application

Step 2 — Environmental Screening:
- Application to Keskkonnaamet for KMH screening if installation is large (> 10 MW) or near sensitive receptors
- Typical outcome for BESS on industrial land: no EIA required
- Chemical hazard assessment: if lithium battery threshold exceeded (varies by specific chemistry; consult Kemikaaliseadus)

Step 3 — Building Permit (Ehitusluba):
- Application to municipality
- Documents required:
  - Site plan showing container layout, access, fire lanes
  - Structural/foundation design for battery containers
  - Electrical installation design (DC battery system, AC inverters, MV/HV connection)
  - Fire safety plan (Tuleohutuse lahendus): sprinkler/suppression system design, smoke detection, emergency procedures
  - Spill containment design (battery electrolyte)
  - Ventilation design (off-gas management for thermal runaway scenarios)
- Building permit from municipality: 30-day statutory; 60 days for complex projects

Step 4 — Electricity License / Registration:
- If discharging > 1 MW: electricity production license from Konkurentsiamet
- Application includes: building permit, Elering/Elektrilevi grid connection agreement
- If < 1 MW: registration notification (teatis) to Konkurentsiamet

Step 5 — Grid Connection:
- Apply to Elering (if > 10 MW or 110 kV connection) or Elektrilevi (if < 10 MW, 20 kV connection)
- BESS grid connection: must demonstrate both charging (consumer) and discharging (generator) compliance with EU Network Codes (RfG 2016/631 for generator mode; DCC 2016/1388 for consumer mode)
- Bidirectional meter required: metered separately for import and export

Step 6 — Fire Authority Inspection:
- Päästeamet (Rescue Board / Fire Authority) inspection required before commissioning
- Inspection verifies: suppression systems, emergency disconnect, fire lanes, signage
- Use permit (kasutusluba) from municipality after Päästeamet clearance

Technical standards for BESS:
- IEC 62619: Safety requirements for secondary lithium cells and batteries
- IEC 62933: Electrical energy storage systems (grid-connected)
- IEC 61000-6-x: Electromagnetic compatibility
- NFPA 855 (US standard, referenced in some EU guidance): Standard for Installation of Stationary Energy Storage Systems
- EN 50604-1: Lead-acid batteries (if applicable)
- Fire safety: Estonian standard EVS 812-7 and Tuleohutuse nõuded hoone projekteerimisel

Specific note — no dedicated BESS regulation:
Estonia has not yet adopted specific BESS regulations defining:
- Safe setback distances from buildings/roads
- Specific fire suppression technology requirements
- Capacity thresholds for environmental permitting
- Revenue/balancing market participation rules for standalone BESS
The competent authority (Päästeamet + municipality) applies general industrial construction standards and fire safety regulations in the absence of BESS-specific rules. Developers should consult Päästeamet and municipality early to align on applicable standards.

=== PART 2: DATA CENTRES (DATAKESKUS) ===

Estonia's data centre sector has grown significantly due to:
- Strong digital infrastructure (Estonia is a leading digital society — e-Residency, i-Voting, X-Road)
- Competitive electricity prices (historically)
- Reliable grid connections
- Cool climate (beneficial for free cooling)

Applicable legislation:
1. Ehitusseadustik (EhS): building permit (ehitusluba) required; all data centre buildings are permanent structures
2. Planeerimisseadus (PlanS): detailplaneering typically required (land use change or large building in new location)
3. KeÜS / KMH-KSH seadus: EIA screening for large data centres (> 100 MW total IT load may trigger screening)
4. Elektrituruseadus (ETS): grid connection agreement with Elering or Elektrilevi; production license if generating electricity (backup generators, CHP)
5. Energiamajanduse korralduse seadus (ESOS): energy efficiency audits for large energy consumers
6. Kemikaaliseadus: diesel fuel storage for backup generators if above threshold quantities

Building permit requirements for data centres:
- Structural design: modular expansion capability, raised floor or hot/cold aisle design
- Electrical installation: redundant power supply (N+1 or 2N UPS), generator backing, MV/HV connection
- Cooling systems: free cooling capability, heat recovery option
- Fire suppression: clean agent systems (FM-200, Inergen, Novec 1230) preferred for server rooms
- Security: access control, CCTV — not regulated by building permit but contractual requirement

Energy efficiency for data centres:
- EU Energy Efficiency Directive (EED 2023) data centre provisions apply from 2024:
  - Data centres > 1 MW must register with national authority (Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium)
  - Annual reporting of PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness), water usage, renewable energy percentage
  - Target PUE: ≤ 1.5 (existing) or ≤ 1.3 (new construction by 2030)
- Estonian ESOS: energy audit obligation for enterprises > 250 employees

Grid connection for data centres:
- Load > 10 MW: direct 110 kV Elering connection typical
- Load 1–10 MW: Elektrilevi 20 kV connection typical
- Redundant connection paths strongly recommended; Elering/Elektrilevi can provide N-1 secure supply at premium
- UPS and battery backup: typically 10–15 minutes hold-up time; > 15 minutes may require BESS grid connection registration

=== PART 3: INDUSTRIAL PROJECTS (TEOLLISUUS) ===

Applicable legislation:
1. Ehitusseadustik (EhS): building permit (ehitusluba) for all industrial buildings
2. Planeerimisseadus (PlanS): detailplaneering if changing land use or new industrial zone
3. KeÜS / KMH-KSH seadus:
   - Complex environmental permit (kompleksluba) for IPPC installations
   - EIA mandatory for large industrial facilities (> 75 MW thermal input, or listed Annex I/II projects)
   - EIA screening for medium installations
4. Kemikaaliseadus (Chemicals Act): chemical storage and handling permit if threshold quantities
5. Tuleohutusseadus (Fire Safety Act): fire safety plan and Päästeamet inspection
6. Töötervishoiu ja tööohutuse seadus (Occupational Health and Safety Act): workplace safety management

IPPC installations (Integrated Pollution Prevention and Control):
- Large combustion plants, chemical manufacturing, waste treatment, metal production — specific thresholds in KeÜS
- Complex environmental permit (kompleksluba) required: covers air, water, waste, soil in one permit
- Competent authority: Keskkonnaamet
- BAT (Best Available Techniques) compliance required; EU BAT Reference Documents (BREFs) apply

Building permit for industrial buildings:
- Fire resistance class: F60 or higher for industrial buildings
- Explosion protection: ATEX zones if flammable substances
- Accessibility: must comply with EVS requirements
- Waste water: connection to municipal sewage or separate treatment plant if industrial effluent
- Noise: acoustic assessment if noise-generating processes near residential areas

=== PART 4: RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS (ASUINRAKENNUS) ===

Applicable legislation:
1. Ehitusseadustik (EhS): building permit (ehitusluba) for all new residential buildings; simplified notification (ehitusteatis) for small additions
2. Planeerimisseadus (PlanS): detailplaneering typically required in urban areas
3. Korteriomandiseadus (Apartment Ownership Act): for apartment buildings, condominium rules
4. Ehitise energiatõhususe miinimumnõuded (Minimum Energy Performance Requirements): EU EPBD implemented through Estonian regulation

Building permit for residential:
- Architectural design by licensed architect (arhitekt)
- Structural design by licensed structural engineer
- Energy performance calculation: must meet Nearly Zero-Energy Building (NZEB) standard
  - New residential buildings: ≤ 100 kWh/m²/year primary energy (from 2020)
  - EPC class B minimum for new construction (class A recommended)
- Fire safety: EVS 812 standards; smoke alarms mandatory; fire escape routes
- Accessibility: disability access requirements for multi-family buildings
- Neighbor consultation: adjacent landowners informed; objections may delay permit

Municipality processing time: 30 days statutory; typically 15–30 days in practice.

=== PART 5: COMMERCIAL BUILDINGS (LIIKERAKENNUS / ÄRIHOONE) ===

Applicable legislation:
1. Ehitusseadustik (EhS): building permit (ehitusluba) required
2. Planeerimisseadus (PlanS): detailplaneering if new location or significant land use change
3. KeÜS: environmental permit if discharges/emissions above thresholds
4. NZEB requirements: commercial buildings must meet energy performance standard (slightly less stringent than residential per square meter)

Building permit for commercial:
- Architectural design (licensed architect)
- Structural design
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical installation designs
- Fire safety plan (Tuleohutuse lahendus) — Päästeamet consultation
- Accessibility: barrier-free access for public buildings (Invahoone design requirements)
- Parking: municipality requirement varies

For large retail/commercial developments:
- Kaubanduslike suurpindade loa nõue: large retail formats may require special commercial development approval from county authority

=== PART 6: AGRICULTURAL BUILDINGS (MAATALOUS) ===

Applicable legislation:
1. Ehitusseadustik (EhS): building permit for permanent agricultural structures; notification (ehitusteatis) for smaller farm buildings under certain thresholds
2. Planeerimisseadus (PlanS): agricultural land zoning is protected; building on agricultural land generally limited to agricultural use buildings only
3. Maaparandusseadus (Land Improvement Act): drainage systems, soil conservation
4. Kemikaaliseadus: fertilizer and pesticide storage if above threshold
5. Loomakaitseseadus (Animal Welfare Act): standards for livestock buildings
6. KeÜS: environmental permit for large livestock farms (IPPC threshold: > 750 sows, > 2,000 pigs, > 40,000 poultry)

Building notification (ehitusteatis) sufficient for:
- Farm buildings < 60 m² floor area
- Greenhouses < 60 m²
- Temporary structures

Full building permit required for:
- Any permanent farm building > 60 m²
- Livestock buildings regardless of size
- Agricultural machinery storage > 60 m²
- Manure storage (slurry tanks, composting areas)

Biogas from agricultural waste:
- Agricultural biogas plants may qualify for renewable energy support
- Electricity production license if > 1 MW output
- Combined heat and power (CHP) encouraged

=== PART 8: KEY AUTHORITIES SUMMARY ===

Kohaliku omavalitsuse volikogu (Municipality council):
- Adopts detailed plans
- Issues building permits and use permits

Keskkonnaamet (Environmental Board):
- EIA decisions
- Complex environmental permits (kompleksluba)
- Nature protection oversight

Päästeamet (Rescue Board / Fire Authority):
- Fire safety inspections
- Approval for BESS, industrial, and large commercial buildings

Konkurentsiamet (Competition Authority):
- Electricity production licenses
- Grid operator regulation

Terviseamet (Health Board):
- Radiation practice licenses (for BESS installations with certain radiation sources — limited)

Põllumajandusamet (Agricultural Board):
- Agricultural land use change approvals
- Oversight of agricultural subsidies

Muinsuskaitseamet (Heritage Board):
- Construction near or in heritage protection zones
