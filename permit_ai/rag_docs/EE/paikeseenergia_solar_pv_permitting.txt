PÄIKESEENERGIA ARENDAMINE EESTIS — SOLAR PV DEVELOPMENT AND PERMITTING IN ESTONIA
Source: Majandus- ja Kommunikatsiooniministeerium, Keskkonnaamet, Konkurentsiamet,
        Elektrituruseadus (ETS), Ehitusseadustik (EhS), Planeerimisseadus (PlanS)
Authority: Kohaliku omavalitsus (Municipality), Keskkonnaamet, Konkurentsiamet, Elektrilevi
Language: Estonian (summary in English for RAG indexing)

=== PART 1: ESTONIA SOLAR ENERGY SECTOR ===

Estonia's solar energy sector is growing rapidly:
- Installed capacity (end 2023): approximately 160 MW (predominantly distributed rooftop)
- National target by 2030: approximately 415 MW
- Growth requirement: approximately 255 MW new capacity needed

Solar resource: Estonia has moderate solar irradiance (approximately 950–1,050 kWh/m²/year global horizontal irradiance), similar to southern Scandinavia. Best months: April–August. Significant snow cover in winter months (November–February) reduces winter generation.

Cost environment: Solar PV costs have fallen significantly; grid parity for commercial installations achieved by 2022–2023 in Estonia.

=== PART 2: PERMITTING — TWO-TRACK SYSTEM ===

Estonia operates a two-track permitting system for solar PV based on project size:

TRACK A — SMALL ROOFTOP SOLAR (Katusele paigaldatav päikeseenergiajaam, < 15 kW / < 50 kW):

For household installations < 15 kW and commercial rooftop installations < 50 kW:
- No electricity production license required (Elektrituruseadus §14(2))
- Simplified registration (teatis) to Konkurentsiamet sufficient
- Building permit NOT required if:
  (a) Panels are mounted flat on existing building roof
  (b) Total additional load on roof is within existing structural capacity
  (c) Panels do not significantly alter the building's external appearance
  (d) Building is not in a heritage conservation zone
- Ehitusteatis (building notification) is sufficient in most cases for rooftop systems
- Electrical installation permit: licensed electrician (atesteeritud elektriinsener) required for grid connection
- Net metering (netomõõtmine) available: excess generation fed to grid at spot market price
- Elektrilevi: simplified grid connection process; Elektrilevi must respond within 30 days

For household installations < 15 kW:
- Registration notification (teatis) to Konkurentsiamet only
- Connect to Elektrilevi distribution; smart meter installed by Elektrilevi
- Annual reporting to Elering (for RES statistics)

TRACK B — UTILITY-SCALE GROUND-MOUNTED SOLAR (Maapealse päikeseenergiajaam, > 50 kW or > 1 MW):

Full permitting process applies:

Phase 1 — Site Selection (6–12 months):
1a. Land ownership or lease agreement
1b. Zoning assessment: is land zoned for energy production or agriculture?
   - Agricultural land conversion: approval from Põllumajandusamet (Agricultural Board) required
   - Protected agricultural land: stronger restrictions; conversion often impossible
1c. Environmental sensitivity screening: Natura 2000, protected habitats, water bodies
1d. Grid connection pre-study: Elering or Elektrilevi feasibility assessment
1e. Shading analysis: horizon profile, row spacing optimization

Phase 2 — Spatial Planning (12–24 months):
2a. Detailplaneering (detailed spatial plan) required if:
   - Land use change from agriculture/forest to energy production
   - Site > 0.5 ha
   - Municipality requires for any PV farm in their jurisdiction
2b. Detailplaneering process:
   - Application to municipality (kohaliku omavalitsuse volikogu)
   - KSH (Strategic Environmental Assessment) screening
   - Public consultation periods
   - Municipal council adoption vote
2c. Some municipalities may waive detailplaneering requirement for small solar farms on already-industrial/brownfield land

Phase 3 — Environmental Impact Assessment (9–18 months, if triggered):
3a. EIA screening by Keskkonnaamet: mandatory EIA if ≥ 50 MW
3b. EIA triggered for < 50 MW if site is:
   - Within or adjacent to Natura 2000 protected area
   - On priority habitat (rare vegetation types)
   - Adjacent to protected watercourse or wetland
   - In landscape protection zone
3c. If EIA required: KMH programm → KMH aruanne → expert review → public hearing → Keskkonnaamet decision (same process as wind, but typically simpler scope)
3d. Most ground-mounted solar farms 1–50 MW: no EIA required if standard agricultural/industrial land; EIA screening typically negative result

Phase 4 — Building Permit (2–4 months):
4a. Application to municipality: ehitusluba (building permit)
4b. Required documents:
   - Site plan with panel layout, row spacing, installation height
   - Structural calculations for mounting systems and foundations
   - Electrical installation design (DC cables, inverters, AC collection, substation)
   - Environmental permit if EIA conditions apply
   - Ground anchoring design (screw piles preferred on agricultural land to avoid foundation disturbance)
4c. Building permit issued by municipality: 30-day statutory timeline

Phase 5 — Electricity Production License:
5a. Application to Konkurentsiamet for license (if > 1 MW)
5b. Required: building permit + Elering/Elektrilevi grid connection agreement (or pre-agreement)
5c. License issued within 30 days

Phase 6 — Grid Connection Agreement:
6a. For systems > 10 MW: Elering 110 kV connection
6b. For systems 1–10 MW: typically Elektrilevi 20 kV connection
6c. Connection conditions (liitumistingimused) issued; connection agreement (liitumisleping) signed
6d. Metering: smart meter installed at grid connection point; production metered

Phase 7 — Construction and Commissioning:
7a. Panel installation, inverter installation, substation works
7b. Elering / Elektrilevi commissioning tests
7c. Use permit (kasutusluba) from municipality
7d. Commercial operation

=== PART 3: KEY TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS ===

Grid connection standards:
- IEC 62109: Safety of power converters for use in photovoltaic power systems
- IEC 62116: Anti-islanding requirements for grid-connected inverters
- EU Network Code RfG 2016/631: Type B (< 1 MW) or Type C (1–75 MW) generator requirements
- Elektrilevi/Elering: power factor correction, voltage regulation requirements

Structural requirements:
- Snow load design: ground-mounted panels must withstand 2.4–3.2 kPa snow load (Estonian standard EVS-EN 1991-1-3)
- Wind load design: 25 m/s reference wind speed coastal/open areas (EVS-EN 1991-1-4)
- Module standards: IEC 61215 (crystalline silicon), IEC 61730 (safety)
- Inverter standards: IEC 62109-1, IEC 62109-2
- DC cable standards: IEC 60228, IEC 60364

Fire safety:
- DC arc fault detection required for utility-scale systems
- Combiner boxes and inverters: IEC 60947-2 or equivalent
- Emergency disconnection within 30 seconds requirement (Päästesüsteemide nõuded — fire brigade requirements)

=== PART 4: AGRICULTURAL LAND PROTECTION ===

Maaparandusseadus (Land Improvement Act) and Põllumajandusliku tegevuse toetuse seadus (Agricultural Support Act):
- Agricultural land in Estonia is classified:
  - Highly productive agricultural land (kõrgväärtuslikud põllumajandusmaa): conversion to energy use strongly restricted
  - Other agricultural land: conversion possible with Põllumajandusamet approval
- Agrivoltaics (dual-use solar — panels over crops): being developed as regulatory concept but not yet formally defined in Estonian law
- Ground-mounted solar on agricultural land requires:
  - Application to Põllumajandusamet for land use change (maaparandus)
  - Justification for removal of agricultural land from production
  - Compensation payment to agricultural support scheme if previously receiving EU CAP payments

=== PART 5: BUILDING INTEGRATED SOLAR (BIPV) ===

For building-integrated solar panels (BIPV — panels replacing roofing material, facade elements):
- Building permit (ehitusluba) required: BIPV is structural modification of building
- If in heritage zone (kultuurimälestis): Muinsuskaitseamet (Heritage Board) approval required
- Fire safety: additional requirements for BIPV systems (access for firefighting, EV disconnection)
- Structural engineer sign-off on roof/facade load bearing capacity

=== PART 6: NET METERING AND PROSUMER RULES ===

Net metering (Netomõõtmine) — Elektrituruseadus §§47-55:
- Available for all generators < 1 MW connected to distribution network
- Electricity consumed from grid: standard consumer tariff
- Electricity exported to grid: spot market price (Nordpool day-ahead) settled monthly by Elektrilevi
- Settlement: Elektrilevi settles net import/export on monthly basis
- Smart meter requirement: Elektrilevi installs smart meter at no additional cost for new PV connections

Prosumer aggregation: Landlord or housing association may collectively install solar PV and distribute generation across multiple tenants — pilot framework being developed in Estonian legislation (2023).

=== PART 7: FINANCIAL SUPPORT ===

Utility-scale (> 1 MW):
- RES competitive auction administered by MKM
- Solar PV technology-neutral in most auction rounds (competes with wind)
- CfD or premium tariff for winning bidders
- Pre-qualification: completed planning, building permit, grid connection agreement

Small commercial (50 kW – 1 MW):
- Investment support programs via Estonian Environmental Investment Centre (KIK)
- EU Structural Funds co-financing available for specific sectors (agriculture, SMEs)

Residential rooftop (< 15 kW):
- Investment grants through KIK (Keskkonnainvesteeringute Keskus)
- State programs: "Solar Energy at Home" support scheme (specific rounds)
- VAT exemption for solar panels installed on residential buildings
